# Security Policy

Thanks for taking the time to help keep OpenPrints and its users safe.

OpenPrints is an open-source, bootstrapped project. We don’t have a formal security team or bug bounty program yet, but we **care a lot** about responsible disclosure and will do our best to respond quickly.

---

## Reporting a Vulnerability

If you believe you’ve found a security issue in:

- the OpenPrints codebase (this repository),
- the OpenPrints infrastructure (e.g. `openprints.dev`, `api.openprints.dev`), or
- any other service operated by the OpenPrints project,

please email:

**`openprints@pm.me`**

Include as much detail as you can:

- a description of the issue and where you found it,
- steps to reproduce,
- any potential impact you see,
- whether you believe it is already being exploited.

You may encrypt reports if you prefer; we can exchange keys after initial contact.

---

## What to Expect

- We aim to **acknowledge your report within 7 days**.
- We’ll keep you updated as we:
  - confirm the issue,
  - work on a fix, and
  - decide when/how to disclose it.
- Once the issue is resolved, we’re happy (with your permission) to **credit you** in release notes or a security acknowledgements section.

Because this is a small, bootstrapped project, timelines can vary, but we will treat good-faith reports as a priority.

---

## Responsible Disclosure Guidelines

To help us handle issues safely:

- **Do not** intentionally access, modify, or delete data that doesn’t belong to you.
- **Do not** perform denial-of-service attacks or other forms of service disruption.
- **Do not** run automated scans that could significantly degrade service for others.
- **Do** use test data and accounts where possible.
- **Do** give us a reasonable opportunity to fix issues before public disclosure.

If you are unsure whether a particular action is acceptable during testing, please ask first at `openprints@pm.me`.

---

## Out of Scope (for now)

Given limited resources, the following are generally out of scope:

- Issues that require physical access to our infrastructure.
- Problems that only affect unsupported or heavily outdated browsers.
- Vulnerabilities that depend entirely on a third-party service misconfiguration outside our control.

If in doubt, feel free to contact us anyway—we’d rather hear about a potential issue than miss something important.

---

Thank you again for helping to keep OpenPrints and its ecosystem safe.
